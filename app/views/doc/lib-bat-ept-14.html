Everyday Problem Test (EPT-14)

The EPT-14 measures problem solving in an everyday context using ecologically valid stimuli (Reinhartz, Strobach, Hyde, Jiang, Jacobsen, Belleville, & von Bastian, submitted for publication). The EPT-14 is an updated and shortened version of the Everyday Problems Test (Willis & Marsiske, 1993) and is currently validated for three languages and countries: English (EPT-14E; UK), French (EPT-14F; Canada), and German (EPT-14G; Germany), with one example item and 14 test items each.

Background

The EPT-14 is based on the Everyday Problems Test (EPT; Willis & Marsiske, 1993); a pen-and-paper-based assessment tool of everyday problem solving. The EPT-14 is sensitive to effects of age and education (Reinhartz et al., submitted for publication). Cronbachâ€™s alpha scores range from α = .49 (EPT-14G), α = .51 (EPT-14E), and α = .55 (EPT-14E; online) to α = .71 (EPT-14F). Test-retest and intraclass reliability coefficients are above r = .60 for online retest intervals of two weeks and three months. The EPT-14 is strongly related to tasks of reasoning and working memory and moderately related to sustained attention, processing speed, and task switching (Reinhartz, et al., ; submitted for publication).

How to Cite

Reinhartz, A., Strobach, T., Hyde, E. R. A., Jiang, S., Jacobsen, T., Belleville, S., & von Bastian, C. C. (submitted for publication). The 14-item Everyday Problems Test (EPT-14): A revised assessment tool for problem solving in an everyday context.
